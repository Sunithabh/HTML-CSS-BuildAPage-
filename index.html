      • REPORT OF MY RESEARCH

      • They have hollow bones
      • They had flying ancestors
      • They have a light weight body
      • They know how to take off and land
      • Four forces of light they use are 'THRUST', 'LIFT', 'DRAG' and 'WEIGHT'

      HYPOTHESIS

      I think that my balloons with least weight will fly higher because my research shows that “a bird needs to be light in order to fly”.
      If the balloon has less weight on it, then it will go highter.

      MATERIALS

      • 6 Balloons (12” balloons)
      • 30 Feathers
      • 6 Clothespins
      • 1 Invisible Tape
      • 1 Marker

      VARIABLES

      1. Controlled Variables
        • Balloons blown up to 30cms long.
        • Clothespins to lock air in balloons.
      2. INDEPENDENT VARIABLES
        • Number of feathers.
      3. RESPONDING VARIABLES
        • How high the balloon reaches.

        4. PROCEDURE

        1. Blow up the balloons to about 30cms long.
        2. Twist the neck of the balloon and clip on the clothespin to lock the air in the balloon.
        3. Draw a face of a bird on the balloon with your marker.
        4. Tape feathers on to the balloons.
          • Balloon 1 will have 0 feathers.
          • Balloon 2 will have 2 feathers, one on each side.
          • Balloon 3 will have 4 feathers, two on each side.
          • Balloon 4 will have 6 feathers, three on each side.
          • Balloon 5 will have 8 feathers, four on each side.
          • Balloon 6 will have 10 feathers, five on each side.
        5. Unclip balloon and toss it upwards in the air.
        6. See how high it goes !!!!
